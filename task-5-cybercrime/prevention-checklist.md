**Stay Safe Online (A Practical Checklist for College Students)**

* **Use strong, unique passwords for every account like mix letters, numbers, and symbols instead of using easy guesses like your name or birthdate.**
* **Turn on two-factor authentication wherever possible, especially for email, banking, and social media accounts.**
* **Never share your OTP, passwords, or UPI PIN with anyone, even if they claim to be from your bank or a trusted service.**
* **Be careful with unknown links or attachments, especially those received through SMS, email, or messaging apps, they can be trap links sent by hackers.**
* **Always check the URL of websites before entering personal details as fake websites often look very similar to real ones.**
* **Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities like logging into online banking portals or making payments.**
* **For UPI safety: Remember that receiving money does not require entering your UPI PIN, only approve requests you fully understand.**
* **For UPI safety: Double-check the name and ID of the person before sending money, and avoid scanning random QR codes.**
* **Keep your apps and phone software updated to protect against security vulnerabilities.**
* **Limit what you share on social media, your personal details can be misused for scams or identity theft.**
* **If you ever face a cyber issue or suspect fraud, report it immediately through the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or call the helpline 1930. Acting quickly can make a big difference.**
